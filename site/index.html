01 · Dropping info

At the surface, everything looks like a fish.

Saved links swim past as loose fragments: tutorials, papers, tools, half-useful threads, repeated tabs, private file URLs, and the occasional treasure pretending to be clutter.

MemoReef starts by importing the mess and turning each source into a readable local Markdown Drop.

Browser exports become files

Every bookmark can become a local Markdown source with URL, title, host, tags, notes, status, and agent brief fields.

No backend required

The pilot app opens from disk. Your archive remains inspectable, portable, editable, and boring in the best possible way.

Signal survives

Hosts, projects, labels, and source quality move into structured frontmatter instead of browser chrome.

Open-source by design

The archive format stays human-readable even if the interface changes later. That is the point. Lock-in is just clutter with a payment form.

02 · Triage current

A floating medusa filters the noise.

Review is not a spreadsheet punishment. MemoReef separates what is duplicate, stale, private, promising, source-worthy, or immediately useful.

03 · Agent octopus

Tentacles extract knowledge without swallowing the archive.

MemoReef prepares source briefs so an agent can reason with context, cite URLs, respect gaps, and avoid confident nonsense. A rare creature, apparently.

The octopus does not replace the library. It reaches through it.

04 · Pearl output

The Octopus surfaces Pearls from your Reef.

Pearls are your highest-value sources — referenced often, shaping a project. MemoReef stores your bookmarks as local, Obsidian-ready Drops, so your AI agents can tag, cluster, and use them as a shared knowledge base for emerging projects.
