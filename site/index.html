Your saved web.
Actually useful again.

MemoReef is for people with hundreds of saved links, scattered notes, and “I know I bookmarked that somewhere” energy. It turns browser exports into a private source library your agents can review, search, cite, and build from.

Stop losing research twice. Import old bookmarks once, then triage them into keep, pearl, or sink instead of pretending folders are a system.
Make AI answer from your sources. Keep source URLs, folder context, tags, and Markdown notes together so local agents can cite the material you already collected.
Own the archive. No new SaaS silo, no monthly hostage situation. Your reef lives as files in your vault.

Buyer: researchers, builders, writers, consultants, and agent operators drowning in saved links but not ready to hand their source memory to another black box.

Import the mess

Start with the ugly reality: a bulk browser bookmark export from Chrome, Brave, Arc, Firefox, or Safari. MemoReef writes each link as a clean Markdown Drop instead of asking you to rebuild your library by hand.

Review fast

Swipe through old saves in short sessions. Sink dead weight, keep useful material, and mark Pearls worth citing or turning into project fuel.

Ask your sources

Agents can search your private reef later, synthesize answers from saved material, and point back to the original links. That is the product. The ocean metaphor is just less depressing than a folder called “misc”.
